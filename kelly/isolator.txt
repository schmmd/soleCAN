Kelly UART galvanic isolator — build plan
=========================================

Status: PLANNED (not yet built/validated). Expected result: OBD-powered
RejsaCAN reads the Kelly with the same ~99% frame success as a floating
USB-powered board.

Why
---
The Kelly KLS7218M is powered from the traction pack, so its V- is not at
chassis potential. The RejsaCAN, powered from the OBD port, is
chassis-referenced (and must stay that way for CAN). Wiring the two grounds
together injects the chassis-to-traction noise into the UART's signal
reference and corrupts frames whenever the board runs on tractor power
(see README.md "Grounding dominates reliability"). A galvanic isolator
removes the ground connection entirely: the Kelly side floats with the
traction pack, the board side stays on chassis, and signals cross an
isolation barrier instead of sharing a reference.

The isolator also replaces the two level-shift hacks: run its Kelly side at
5 V and it natively reads the Kelly's 5 V-TTL Tx and drives the Kelly's Rx
at a full 5 V, while the board side regenerates everything at 3.3 V. The
~1-2.2 kOhm series resistor on the RX line is no longer needed.

Part choice
-----------
ADuM1201 dual-channel digital isolator, one channel in each direction —
exactly the UART topology. 2500 V RMS isolation, >=1 Mbps (50x headroom
over the link's 19200 Bd). Sold on ~$3-8 breakout boards, often labeled
"CJMCU-1201". Any speed grade (A/B/C suffix) works.

Do NOT substitute:
  - ISO1540 / ADuM1250 / ADuM1251 — those are I2C isolators (bidirectional
    open-drain with arbitration); driving them with push-pull UART fights
    their design.
  - TI ISO7721 is a fine equivalent if a breakout of it is easier to get.

Isolated-side power: 12 V -> 5 V
--------------------------------
The isolated (Kelly) side needs a supply referenced to the Kelly's V-.
That is what SM-4P pin 1 (red, ~12 V out of the controller) is for — it is
how Kelly's own Bluetooth dongle powers itself. Never wire the 12 V to the
isolator directly; regulate it down:

  - 78L05 (TO-92) + 0.33 uF ceramic on IN and 0.1 uF on OUT, both right at
    the regulator. Load is a few mA, so dissipation is ~40 mW — no heat
    sink. A pre-made AMS1117-5.0 breakout module is an equivalent
    no-soldering option.
  - Do not use a switching buck module: at 5 mA there is no efficiency
    argument, and it puts converter hash next to the UART lines.
  - Optional but useful: LED + 1 kOhm across the 5 V rail. It lights only
    when the controller is powering pin 1, so it doubles as a
    "Kelly is awake" indicator.

Wiring
------
        KELLY SIDE (floating)      | barrier |      BOARD SIDE (chassis)
                                   |         |
 red ---> 78L05 ---> 5V ---> VDD1 -| ADuM    |- VDD2 <--- 3V3
 black --------------------> GND1 -|  1201   |- GND2 <--- GND
 green (Kelly Tx) ---------> VIB  -| breakout|- VOB  ---> GPIO47 (RX)
 blue  (Kelly Rx) <--------- VOA  -|         |- VIA  <--- GPIO48 (TX)

Pin map (datasheet pin numbers — side 1 carries VOA/VIB, side 2 VOB/VIA;
channel A runs board->Kelly, channel B Kelly->board):

  Kelly side (isolated, side 1)        Board side (chassis, side 2)
  VDD1 (1) <- 5 V from regulator       VDD2 (8) <- RejsaCAN 3V3
  GND1 (2) <- Kelly black (V-)         GND2 (5) <- board GND
  VIB  (4) <- Kelly green (Tx), input  VOB  (6) -> GPIO47 (RX), output
  VOA  (3) -> Kelly blue (Rx), output  VIA  (7) <- GPIO48 (TX), input

There is no longer any wire between Kelly V- and board GND, and no series
resistor anywhere on the link.

Note the ADuM1201's input thresholds scale with each side's own VDD, so
the Kelly side must actually be powered at 5 V; at 3.3 V it would
under-drive the Kelly's Rx again.

Packaging
---------
Build it as an inline dongle on the harness, isolation barrier down the
middle of one small (~3 x 4 cm) perfboard:

 1. Regulator + caps + LED on the Kelly third, ADuM1201 breakout across
    the middle, board-side connector on the other third. Iron rule: no
    wire, trace, or component leg crosses the middle except the isolator
    itself — that gap IS the isolation.
 2. A 4-pin connector (JST-XH or screw terminals) on each end makes the
    dongle removable. Kelly side: red/green/blue/black in SM-4P harness
    order. Board side: 3V3, GND, GPIO47, GPIO48.
 3. Twist the pairs in the Kelly-side cable (green+black, blue+red), keep
    that run short, and route it away from the pump phase leads. The
    board-side run is noise-immune by construction.
 4. Zip-tie both cables through holes in the perfboard corners for strain
    relief, then sleeve the whole board in large-diameter heatshrink or a
    small project box, LED visible.
 5. Label both ends (KELLY / REJSACAN) — the dongle looks symmetric.

Bill of materials (~$10)
------------------------
  - ADuM1201 breakout board ("CJMCU-1201")
  - 78L05 + 0.33 uF + 0.1 uF ceramics (or AMS1117-5.0 module)
  - LED + 1 kOhm resistor
  - perfboard ~3 x 4 cm, two 4-pin JST-XH pairs (or screw terminals),
    heatshrink, twisted hookup wire

Behavior notes
--------------
  - The ADuM1201 is fail-safe high: with the tractor off (Kelly side
    unpowered) GPIO47 sees a clean idle-high line, not garbage, so the
    firmware's existing stale-out handles key-off with no code changes.
  - Verification: flash the dashboard firmware (-DENABLE_KELLY
    -DKELLY_DEBUG), power the board from the OBD port, run the pump, and
    watch /json kelly_dbg — frames_ok should climb at ~99% of polls, as it
    does on floating USB power.
