NOTES — Solectrac CAN-bus reverse-engineering findings
======================================================

These notes record what we learned about *this specific tractor* and *these
specific captures*. The README covers the parser itself in generic terms;
this file is where the experimental, observational, and tentative material
lives. Numbers below come from the captures in this directory and are
subject to revision when better data or vendor documentation arrives.


Tractor / pack context
----------------------

* Tractor: Solectrac electric tractor (~70 V class).
* Pack chemistry: NMC (vendor screenshot labels it "NiCoMn"), confirming
  what the OCV-curve shape already suggested.
* Pack topology: **20 series cells** (s = 20), now CONFIRMED both from
  per-cell PGN coverage and from the vendor BMS GUI ("Cells in series: 20",
  "Online cell count: 20"). Module-temperature frames reserve up to 80
  channels across F155..F15E but the pack has **7 temperature probes**
  ("Temp. probe count: 7" / "Online temp. count: 7" in the GUI) — anything
  beyond the first 7 channels in `temps.csv` is padding/noise.
* Pack capacity / ratings (from the GUI, not derivable from CAN alone):
    - Rated capacity: 300 Ah
    - Rated total voltage: 72.0 V nominal
    - Rated discharge current: 300 A
    - Rated DC charge: 78 A
    - Rated AC charge: 39 A
* SOC at time of capture: ~40% (operator-reported).
* Ambient: ~55 °F (~13 °C); tractor was outdoors.
* The PTO/drive inverter and motor controller do **not** appear on the bus
  we logged. The diagnostic port we tapped is the BMS/charger CAN bus
  (sometimes called the "accessory" or "vehicle" bus). The dashboard is
  almost certainly a dual-bus gateway between this bus and the drivetrain
  bus, which would explain how it can show RPM/temps/SOC/etc. without all
  of those signals being visible here.


Captures used
-------------

* `continuous.csv`  — long resting baseline at ~40% SOC. Useful for noise
                      floor, standby draw, and temperature stability.
* `startingup.csv`  — wake-up transition. Vehicle-controller heartbeat
                      transitions from 0x00 (init) to 0x0C (ready).
* `driving.csv`     — ~30 s of normal driving with no implements.
* `dph.csv`         — driving *plus hydraulics*. Same drive activity as
                      `driving.csv` with the hydraulic circuit active in
                      addition. Despite the name "driving plus hydraulics"
                      this capture also contains substantial idle/parked
                      time that drags its mean current down — compare
                      *peak* / high-percentile values, not means, when
                      contrasting it with `driving.csv`.
* `charging.csv`    — charging from ~40% SOC.
* `data.csv`        — *excluded*. Contains additional injected/unrelated
                      OBD-II "Tester Present" traffic the operator was
                      generating at the same time. Not part of the BMS/
                      charger conversation we're trying to characterize.

--------------------------------------------------

| SA   | Role inferred                           | Notes                          |
|------|------------------------------------------|--------------------------------|

|      |                                          | F107/F108/F113../F155.. set.   |
| 0xE5 | On-board charger                         | Talks FF50 telemetry.          |

|      |                                          | charger SA 0xE5).              |

|      |                                          | byte-0 transitions 0x00 → 0x0C |

PGN decode notes (BMS, SA = 0xF3)
---------------------------------

All scalings below were derived empirically. Where a scaling is consistent
with sane physical units across multiple captures it's marked CONFIRMED;
otherwise it's TENTATIVE.

F113..F13C — "BMS cell voltages N..N+3"
    8 bytes = 4 × big-endian uint16 in millivolts.
    F113 = cells 0..3, F114 = cells 4..7, ... F13C = cells 164..167.
    Only the first 20 cells of F113..F117 have nonzero / sensible
    values for this pack; the rest are 0xFFFF (sentinel) or 0.
    CONFIRMED: BE u16 mV. Cells read ~3.6–3.7 V at ~40% SOC.

F155..F15E — "BMS module temps 8N..8N+7"
    8 bytes = 8 × uint8 with J1939 +40 °C offset (i.e. raw 53 = 13 °C).
    F155 = channels 0..7, F156 = 8..15, ... F15E = 72..79.
    Most channels are 0xFF (not present); a small number reflect ambient
    and were what let us pin down the +40 °C convention.
    CONFIRMED: u8, offset 40 °C.

F102F3 — "BMS cell min/max summary"
    Carries pack-wide cell statistics. We use:
      * bytes 1..2 (BE u16) = max-cell mV
      * bytes 3..4 (BE u16) = min-cell mV
      * byte 5            = max-cell **number, 1-based** (CONFIRMED)
      * byte 6            = min-cell **number, 1-based** (CONFIRMED)
      * byte 8            = status/flag bits
      * spread = max - min (mV) — derived
      * a coarse pack-voltage estimate (sum of 20 × cell average), kept
        as a sanity check
    CONFIRMED for max/min/spread.

    Indexing convention (resolved by row-order correlation between F102
    frames and surrounding F113..F117 cell-voltage frames in
    recorded-data/charging.csv):

      * byte5/byte6 are **1-based** cell numbers as the BMS GUI displays
        them (e.g. "Max cell #19").
      * To map to the parser's 0-based `cell_index` in cells.csv,
        subtract 1: BMS cell N = decoder cell_index N-1.
      * Cross-check: in the smallest-spread frames (3 mV) of
        recorded-data/charging.csv, F102 reports byte5=16, byte6=1,
        max=3649 mV, min=3646 mV. The contemporaneous per-cell snapshot
        shows decoder cell_index 15 at 3649 mV (= BMS cell #16 ✓) and
        decoder cell_index 0 as the lowest at 3645 mV (= BMS cell #1 ✓).
      * The vendor screenshot's "Max cell #19 / Min cell #2" therefore
        corresponds to decoder cell_index 18 and cell_index 1.

    Minor caveat: F102's reported min_mv is occasionally 1 mV higher
    than the actual lowest voltage seen in the per-cell PGN snapshot
    around it (e.g. 3646 vs 3645). Two plausible explanations: brief
    timing skew between the F102 summary computation and the per-cell
    sweep, or light filtering inside the BMS. The *index* still
    correctly points at the right cell, just with a slightly stale mV.

    Tie-breaking observation: when several cells are tied at the max,
    the BMS appears to report the lowest-index winner. Across 124
    smallest-spread frames where decoder cell_index {15, 17, 18} were
    all tied at 3649 mV, F102 byte5 was always 16 (= cell #16, i.e.
    decoder index 15, the lowest of the tied set).

    Smallest spread observed across all captures: **3 mV** (124 frames,
    all in recorded-data/charging.csv).

F100F3 — "BMS pack status (incl. |I|)"
    * Bytes 2..3: a 16-bit field that scales like a pack-voltage proxy.
      Magnitude is plausible but the exact LSB is TENTATIVE.
    * Byte 4: pack-current magnitude at 0.1 A/bit — this is the |I|
      reading the script labels as "pack current". CONFIRMED for
      magnitude (matches charger current during charging within
      rounding); the *sign* (charge vs discharge) is **not** carried in
      this byte. Direction must be inferred from context (vehicle state,
      charger active, etc.) until we identify the sign bit.
    * Other bytes likely include SOC/SOH/temperature summaries; not yet
      decoded.

F104F3 — "BMS temp min/max summary"
    Pack-wide hottest/coldest module-temperature summary, analogous to
    F102 for cells. Not currently parsed into its own CSV.

F106F3 — "BMS state (charger-dependent)"
    Periodic frame whose contents differ between idle, driving, and
    charging captures. Likely BMS state machine + flags. Not decoded.

F107F3 — "BMS current/voltage limits"
    Looks like the standard J1939 BMS "limits" frame (charge current
    limit, discharge current limit, voltage limits). Bytes are stable
    when SOC is stable, change near limits. Not decoded.

F108F3 — "BMS aux status"
    Auxiliary/contactor/heater status flags. Not decoded.


PGN decode notes (charger, SA = 0xE5)
-------------------------------------

FF50E5 — "Charger telemetry (V, A)"
    Proprietary B frame from the on-board charger.
      * byte 0 = status / mode (0 when idle, nonzero when charging)
      * bytes 1..2 (LE u16) = output voltage at ~1/3 V/bit
        (raw 0x00DC ≈ 73.3 V matches the pack at the time)
      * bytes 3..4 (LE u16) = output current at 0.1 A/bit
    CONFIRMED for V and A magnitudes during charging; status byte
    semantics (beyond "0 = idle") are TENTATIVE.


PGN decode notes (vehicle controller, SA = 0xD0)
------------------------------------------------

F100D0 — "VC heartbeat"
    Note: same PGN (F100) as the BMS pack-status frame, but a different
    *source*. The PGN namespace is global; it's the SA that disambiguates.
    Observed byte-0 values:
      * 0x00 — "init" (early in startingup.csv, before key/wake completes)
      * 0x0C — "ready" (steady state once tractor is awake)
    The transition is sharp and aligns with the operator-described
    wake-up event, which is what gives us confidence in the labels.
    Other bytes change but haven't been decoded.


Vendor BMS GUI screenshot — provenance and ground-truth values
--------------------------------------------------------------

A screenshot of the vendor BMS GUI (an "Intelligent Battery Management
System" tool, opened in Excel as "300Ah Battery - Excel") has been used as
out-of-band ground truth. Key observations:

* Footer marking: **"Classification | ESCORTS-INTERNAL"**. The document
  originated inside Escorts (now Escorts Kubota Ltd, Faridabad, India),
  not Solectrac. The BMS is therefore a third-party module that Escorts
  also uses on their own electric tractor program — likely the same OEM
  pack-and-BMS bundle is supplied to both. If a vendor protocol PDF can
  be located via that route, it would resolve most of the remaining
  TENTATIVE assumptions.
* GUI tabs visible: System overview, Real time info, Parameter
  Calibration, Force control, Firmware upgrading, History data.
* Firmware version: 2.1.8 (3401e6a).
* Snapshot timestamp: 2022/3/23 14:19:06 UTC+08, uptime 2m52s. Time
  zone and styling indicate a Chinese OEM authored the GUI; the GUI also
  exposes a "DTU" (cellular modem) panel showing GSM/GPRS state, which is
  consistent with that OEM family but irrelevant to the CAN decode.
* Snapshot state — useful as fixed reference points:
    - Running mode: "Calibrating"
    - Wake-up signal: "KL15" (automotive ignition-line nomenclature)
    - SOC: 0.0 %  (Initial SOC: 50.0 %; SOH: 100.0 %)
    - Pack voltage: 69.2 V; pack current: 0.0 A
    - Avg cell volt: 3.458 V; avg temp: 28 °C
    - Max cell: #19 at 3.462 V; Min cell: #2 at 3.452 V; spread 0.010 V
    - All 7 temp probes at 28 °C
    - Charger: Not Connected / Not Charging / 0.0 V / 0.0 A
    - Active alarm: "LowSoc", level 2, count 1
* Cross-check: avg cell 3.458 V × 20 cells = 69.16 V matches displayed
  pack voltage 69.2 V — confirms the parser's `pack_v_estimate = 20 ×
  mean cell mV` formula.

Implications for decoding work the screenshot does *not* close out:

* Running-mode vocabulary includes at least "Calibrating" plus the
  parser's current "init" (0x00) and "ready" (0x0C). "Charging",
  "Discharging", "Fault", "Sleep" are likely. The full enumeration of
  F100D0 byte 0 (and probably an analogous BMS-side mode byte in
  F106F3) cannot be filled in from a single snapshot.
* SOC and SOH are not yet decoded from any captured PGN. Candidate
  homes: F104, F106, F107, F108. With SOC=0% and SOH=100% in the
  screenshot, the values are not specific enough to grep for; richer
  states are needed to pin them down.
* Alarms are reported with severity ("lvl 2"), implying a multi-level
  alarm word — likely in F108. Not yet decoded.
* Wake-up signal source ("KL15") implies an ignition-status bit
  somewhere in the BMS broadcasts. Not yet identified.

Decoder errata exposed by the screenshot:

* The **"Cells in parallel: 20"** field on the GUI almost certainly
  refers to parallel groups internal to the pack (20S20P × ~15 Ah cells
  ≈ 300 Ah), not anything visible on CAN. Don't confuse with series
  count.
* The 300 A rated discharge limit is **inconsistent** with the parser's
  current "F100 byte 4 at 0.1 A/bit" if byte 4 is uint8 — that scaling
  caps at 25.5 A. Either:
    - the LSB is actually closer to ~1.25 A/bit (full scale 318 A), or
    - pack current is a 16-bit field elsewhere in F100, or
    - byte 4 is something other than pack current.
  This contradicts the existing CONFIRMED note on F100 byte 4. The
  empirical match against charger current during charging.csv (~tens
  of A range) doesn't rule out a larger LSB, since charging never
  approaches 300 A. Re-evaluate with a driving capture that pulls real
  load. Status of F100 byte 4 should probably be downgraded from
  CONFIRMED to TENTATIVE pending that check.


Source-CSV timestamp column is unusable
---------------------------------------

The "Time Stamp" column in recorded-data/*.csv is essentially a single
constant value across an entire capture (e.g. `62336000` is shared by
nearly all 70,920 rows of recorded-data/charging.csv, with only sporadic
increments to `62338000`). After parsing, this propagates into the
output CSVs:

    cells.csv         — 8960 rows, 1 unique timestamp
    cell_summary.csv  — 4470 rows, 1 unique timestamp

Consequences:

* Any timestamp-based join between cells / temps / cell_summary / etc.
  is meaningless.
* Time-derivative quantities (dV/dt, dT/dt, frame rate, integrated Ah)
  cannot be computed from these captures.
* The only reliable synchronization between F102 and the surrounding
  F113..F117 cell-voltage stream is **row order within a single source
  CSV**. The F102 ↔ cell-snapshot correlation that established 1-based
  indexing was done that way.

Whatever logger produced these CSVs is either not configured to emit
real per-frame timestamps, or is emitting them in a unit / field the
parser is not consuming. Worth investigating before recording further
captures, since fixing this at the source is the cheapest way to
unlock all time-domain analysis.


Confirmed quantitative findings
-------------------------------

* Resting / standby pack draw (continuous.csv): on the order of
  100–200 W. Consistent with BMS + dash + accessory loads, no traction.
* Driving (driving.csv): peak draw on the order of ~1 kW; typical
  driving current well under nameplate.
* Driving + hydraulics (dph.csv): hydraulic actuation adds clearly
  visible current spikes on top of the drive baseline; peak combined
  draw on the order of ~1.8 kW. The mean of dph.csv is *lower* than
  driving.csv only because dph.csv includes more idle/parked time —
  per-event peaks are higher in dph.
* Charging (charging.csv): charger output on the order of ~1.5 kW into
  the pack at this SOC. Pack |I| reported by F100 byte 4 matches
  charger current from FF50E5 within rounding, which is what cross-
  validates the 0.1 A/bit scaling.
* Cell spread at rest: tens of mV. No outlier cells observed.
* No active DM1 trouble codes during any capture (FECA frames are
  emitted but their SPN/FMI fields are inactive).
* Temperatures: all populated channels read 12–15 °C, consistent with
  the ~13 °C ambient. No self-heating signature in any capture (none
  of these runs are long or hard enough to warm the pack).


Open questions / future work
----------------------------

* F100 bytes 2..3 voltage scaling — the LSB is TENTATIVE. Capturing a
  full charge from low SOC to 100% should let us pin it down by fitting
  to the known charger voltage from FF50.
* Pack-current sign — F100 byte 4 is magnitude only. Look for a sign
  bit elsewhere in F100 (possibly in the status/flags byte) or for
  sign in F107/F108.
* F106 / F107 / F108 byte-level decoding — not yet attempted. Compare
  values across charging-vs-idle-vs-driving captures to isolate which
  byte is which.
* Drivetrain bus — find the second CAN connector. The dashboard
  receives RPM/motor telemetry that is *not* on this bus, so there is
  another CAN segment somewhere on the vehicle. Plausible options:
    - a separate diagnostic port (look for one near the controller)
    - a 2-pin service tap (small Deutsch connector)
    - a backshell tap on the dash harness
  Once tapped, expect to see SAE-standard PGNs like 61444 (EEC1, RPM),
  65262 (Engine Temperature), and similar.
* Full-SOC capture — operator agreed to capture a continuous file at
  100% SOC. That data should refine the cell-voltage range and
  validate the pack-voltage scaling.
* Confirm whether dashboard is the bus gateway, or whether a dedicated
  gateway ECU sits between the two segments. Either is plausible.
* Identify the BMS vendor via the Escorts trail. The GUI's
  "ESCORTS-INTERNAL" classification narrows the candidate OEM list
  considerably; locating a protocol PDF would close out most TENTATIVE
  items at once.
* Re-record with real per-frame timestamps. The current source CSVs'
  "Time Stamp" column is effectively constant, blocking all
  time-derivative analysis (see "Source-CSV timestamp column is
  unusable" section). Find the toggle in whichever logger produced
  these files, or switch loggers, before the next capture session.
* Re-validate F100 byte 4 current scaling against a capture that
  actually pulls high traction current. The 0.1 A/bit assumption is
  inconsistent with the 300 A rated discharge limit if byte 4 is uint8.
* Hunt SOC / SOH / alarms in F104 / F106 / F107 / F108. The vendor
  GUI snapshot gave us a vocabulary (LowSoc lvl 2, Calibrating,
  KL15, etc.) but not enough states to disambiguate byte positions
  from a single snapshot.


Confidence summary
------------------

CONFIRMED:
    cell-voltage scaling (BE u16 mV)
    module-temp scaling (u8 + 40 °C)
    pack topology: 20S, 7 temp probes (vendor GUI)
    pack chemistry: NMC (vendor GUI: "NiCoMn")
    charger output V (FF50 LE u16 @ 1/3 V/bit)
    charger output A (FF50 LE u16 @ 0.1 A/bit)
    vehicle-controller heartbeat init/ready states (F100D0 byte 0)
    F102 byte5 = max-cell number, 1-based
    F102 byte6 = min-cell number, 1-based
    pack-V estimate formula (20 × mean cell mV) cross-validates against
        the GUI's displayed pack voltage

TENTATIVE / GUESSED:
    F102 byte 8 (flags) semantics
    F100 voltage scaling (bytes 2..3)
    F100 byte 4 current scaling — matched charger current during
        charging.csv at 0.1 A/bit, but inconsistent with the 300 A
        rated discharge limit if byte 4 is uint8. Re-check with a
        capture that draws real traction load; likely either a
        larger LSB or a 16-bit field instead.
    FF50 status-byte semantics beyond 0/nonzero
    All of F104 / F106 / F107 / F108

UNKNOWN:
    pack-current sign
    SOC / SOH host PGN
    alarm word location (screenshot showed "LowSoc lvl 2")
    KL15 / wake-up status bit location
    full enumeration of running-mode codes (at least Calibrating, plus
        init/ready already known on F100D0)
    SA 0x12 / 0xCA roles beyond the surface descriptions above
    everything on the (un-tapped) drivetrain bus
    why the source CSV "Time Stamp" column is effectively constant
